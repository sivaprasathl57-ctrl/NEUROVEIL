⚖ Cyber Crime Sections

Complete Information Technology Act cyber crime sections, their purpose and applicable offences.

Section 43

Unauthorized Access

Unauthorized access, downloading data, virus attacks, damaging systems and disrupting networks.

Protects computer systems and digital resources from illegal activities.
Section 65

Tampering Source Code

Destroying, modifying or concealing computer source code illegally.

Protects software source codes and digital evidence.
Section 66

Computer Related Offence

Hacking, unauthorized access and illegal computer activities.

Punishes cyber criminals involved in hacking offences.
Section 66B

Receiving Stolen Device

Receiving stolen computer devices or communication equipment knowingly.

Prevents illegal possession of stolen digital devices.
Section 66C

Identity Theft

Using passwords, ATM cards, Aadhaar or digital signatures illegally.

Protects users from identity theft and impersonation.
Section 66D

Online Cheating

Cheating through fake calls, fake websites and impersonation online.

Punishes cyber fraud and online scams.
Section 66E

Violation of Privacy

Capturing or sharing private images without permission.

Protects privacy and personal dignity online.
Section 66F

Cyber Terrorism

Cyber attacks against national security and government systems.

Protects national infrastructure from cyber terrorism.
Section 67

Obscene Content

Publishing or transmitting obscene material electronically.

Prevents spread of harmful obscene content online.
Section 67A

Sexually Explicit Content

Publishing sexually explicit videos or images online.

Controls explicit adult digital content.
Section 67B

Child Abuse Material

Publishing or browsing child sexual abuse content online.

Protects children from online exploitation.
Section 68

Failure to Comply

Failure to follow directions issued by cyber authorities.

Ensures compliance with cyber security laws.
Section 69

Interception & Monitoring

Government authority to monitor and decrypt information.

Used for national security and cyber investigations.
Section 69A

Blocking Public Access

Government can block harmful websites or online content.

Protects citizens from dangerous digital content.
Section 69B

Traffic Data Monitoring

Monitoring internet traffic data for cyber security purposes.

Strengthens national cyber security systems.
Section 70

Protected Systems

Unauthorized access to protected government systems.

Protects critical government infrastructures.
Section 70A

Critical Information Infrastructure

Protection of critical national information infrastructure.

Strengthens cyber protection for important national systems.
Section 70B

CERT-In

Indian Computer Emergency Response Team cyber incident handling.

Handles cyber attack response and cyber security alerts.
Section 71

Misrepresentation

Providing false information for obtaining digital certificates.

Prevents misuse of electronic approvals and certificates.
Section 72

Breach of Confidentiality

Unauthorized disclosure of confidential electronic information.

Protects sensitive digital information and privacy.
Section 72A

Disclosure of Information

Sharing personal information without user consent illegally.

Protects personal data and confidentiality.
Section 73

False Digital Signature

Publishing false digital signature certificates fraudulently.

Protects authenticity of digital signatures.
Section 74

Fraudulent Certificate Publication

Publishing digital signature certificates for fraudulent purposes.

Prevents fraud involving digital certifications.
